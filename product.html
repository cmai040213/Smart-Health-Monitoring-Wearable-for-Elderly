Introduction

Elderly people who live alone may struggle to cope with a sudden fall or health problems without immediate assistance. Although various types of monitoring devices are available on the market, they are often expensive or difficult for seniors to use.

Solution

The goal of this project is to create a simple wearable device that can detect falls, track heartbeats, and determine locations at a low cost, while maintaining essential functions to improve the lives of the elderly.

Major Compoents

Arduino R4 Wifi
MAX30102
MPU6050
NEO-6M

System Design & Method

Heart rate is collected when the device is worn on the wrist using the MAX30102. MPU6050 collects acceleration and gyroscope data for detecting sudden changes related to falls, combined with the formula in Table 4 and the reference acceleration values in Table 6 from SisFall: A Fall and Movement Dataset (1). The Arduino Uno R4 WiFi processed raw sensor data, NEO-6M GPS, and sent it through WiFi to a website dashboard, where fall status, heart rate, and location were displayed.

Result

The prototype shows that MPU6050 can detect falls based on acceleration and gyroscope. The MAX30102 can detect heartbeats and the measured BPM values matched the expected range for healthy resting or light-activity adults as defined by the American Heart Association (2); however, it still requires time to stabilize and begin reading due to weak light. Although NEO-6M can track outdoor locations, it cannot determine indoor locations. Overall, the utilization of these sensors combined could create a low-cost alternative device readily available for elderly users.
With a second version, the design will be improved to make it more comfortable, enhance the GPS to obtain indoor locations, and well as a stronger heart rate sensor.
